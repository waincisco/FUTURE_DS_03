## Bank Marketing Funnel Analysis

## Project Overview
This project analyzes a bank marketing campaign dataset to understand the marketing funnel from lead generation to customer conversion. The objective is to identify factors that influence deposit subscription and improve marketing effectiveness.

## Dataset
The dataset contains approximately 45,000 marketing contacts conducted by a bank to promote term deposit products.

Key variables include:
- Customer demographics (age, job, education)
- Financial indicators (balance, loans)
- Marketing details (contact method, campaign attempts)
- Call duration
- Campaign outcomes

## Tools Used
- Power BI
- Data Cleaning
- Funnel Analysis
- Marketing Analytics

## Key Metrics
- Total Leads: 45,000
- Total Customers: 5,289
- Conversion Rate: 11.7%
- Average Call Duration: 258 seconds

## Key Insights

### 1. Engagement Duration Drives Conversions
Successful subscriptions are associated with significantly longer call durations, suggesting that deeper customer engagement increases conversion probability.

### 2. Mobile Channels Perform Best
Cellular contact methods account for the majority of successful conversions, indicating that mobile outreach is the most effective communication channel.

### 3. Campaign Fatigue Reduces Conversions
Conversion rates decline as the number of campaign attempts increases, suggesting that excessive follow-up may reduce customer responsiveness.

### 4. Customer Demographics Influence Response
Certain professional groups such as management and technicians show higher subscription counts, indicating potential opportunities for targeted marketing.

## Recommendations
- Prioritize mobile communication channels for outreach.
- Encourage longer and more engaging customer interactions.
- Limit excessive campaign attempts to avoid customer fatigue.
- Focus marketing efforts on high-performing customer segments.
